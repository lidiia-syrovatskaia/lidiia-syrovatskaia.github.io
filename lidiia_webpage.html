I’m a QA Engineer with a proficiency in manual testing of web applications across both frontend and backend, ensuring reliability, usability, and overall product quality.

Alongside manual testing, I started to move into JavaScript-based test automation with a practical, value-driven approach, identifying where it delivers the most value and how it can strengthen manual testing rather than substitute it. Career Path:

My Journey into Quality Assurance With over 15 years of experience as an analyst in international trade, I managed complex import and export operations, ensuring products met regulatory requirements and passed through customs without delays or fines. In this role, I treated each shipment like a project—analyzing every parameter, assessing risks, preparing documentation, and making critical decisions. Much like software testing, I needed to examine every aspect of a product, anticipate potential issues, and ensure a smooth “release” to the market. This experience honed my analytical thinking, attention to detail, and ability to balance technical constraints with stakeholder expectations.

When I relocated to Spain and decided to transition into a new field, I searched for a role that matched my personality, skills, and professional experience. Quality Assurance immediately stood out. The responsibilities—validating functionality, testing across layers, assessing risks, and ensuring a high-quality release—mirrored what I had been doing in international trade for years. The difference was exciting: now the “product” was software, but the principles of rigorous testing, careful analysis, and informed decision-making remained the same.

Since 2025, I have built a strong foundation in QA through hands-on experience with both manual and automation testing. At Delekhomes, I performed end-to-end testing, regression, smoke, and exploratory testing while designing role-based test scenarios and validating APIs. Currently at FoxYou, I contribute to a real-time English-speaking platform, building automated regression suites with JavaScript, Playwright, and CI/CD workflows, while collaborating with developers, product managers, and stakeholders to ensure release stability and quality.

My Approach:

My approach to QA is rooted in a systematic, analytical mindset. I believe in testing thoroughly, documenting clearly, and communicating effectively with all stakeholders. My goal is to ensure that every release is stable, reliable, and meets user expectations.

My background in complex, detail-driven processes gives me a unique perspective as a QA engineer. I approach each project as a comprehensive system, analyzing all layers, identifying risks, and making balanced decisions to deliver reliable, high-quality software. Transitioning into QA was not just a career move — it was a natural evolution of the skills and mindset I developed over nearly two decades of analytical work.

Transitioning from international trade to QA felt natural because the principles are the same. In both fields, I needed to analyze complex systems, evaluate risks, and make informed decisions that could have significant consequences if overlooked. Now, in QA, I get to do this with software — ensuring each feature, workflow, and system behaves correctly — and I’ve found a career where my experience, skills, and mindset truly align.
